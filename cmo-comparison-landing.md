## Use Case Illustrations

### Use Case 1: "I always forget to follow up"

**Monica:** You'd create a reminder manually, then mark it complete.
**Twenty:** Built for B2B sales pipelines — not personal life.
**SOCOS:** Your AI agent detects stale contacts (no interaction in 30 days) and *automatically* drafts a check-in message. You approve or edit before it sends.

---

### Use Case 2: "I met someone great at a conference and can't remember their name"

**Monica:** Add them manually, hope you remember to tag them as "Conference Contact."
**Twenty:** No personal context at all — it's sales software.
**SOCOS:** Add them in 15 seconds. Your enrichment agent pulls their LinkedIn and GitHub. Your gamification agent reminds you to reconnect in 7 days. XP earned.

---

### Use Case 3: "My partner and I want to share contacts for mutual friends"

**Monica:** Shared vault feature exists, but not designed for couples.
**Twenty:** Team/company-focused — not personal relationships.
**SOCOS:** Shared vaults for couples, families, or friend groups. Fine-grained permissions. Celebration packs sync across all members.

## The Differentiation Pitch

> Monica is beautiful but passive. You do all the work.
> Twenty is powerful but business-first. Your personal life isn't a sales pipeline.
> SOCOS is the first CRM that treats your relationships like the valuable thing they are — by putting AI agents in charge of the system so you can focus on the humans.
