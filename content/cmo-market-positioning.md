## Positioning Statement

> **SOCOS is the AI-powered personal CRM that turns relationship-building into a rewarding game — for people who want to grow their circle, not their pipeline.**

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: The Relationship Intentionalist
- **Age:** 25–45
- **Life stage:** Career transition, new city, freelance/entrepreneurial phase, or anyone experiencing relationship drift
- **Behavior:** Uses productivity tools,有一定 self-hosting comfort, active on Twitter/LinkedIn, builds in public
- **Pain:** Wants to stay in touch but lacks a system. Has tried Notion templates, spreadsheets, Apple Contacts — nothing sticks.
- **Trigger moment:** Realizes they've lost touch with someone important, or moved somewhere new and want to be intentional
- **Willingness to pay:** Free / self-hosted; will pay for convenience tier
- **Tech stack:** Notion, Linear, Raycast, Obsidian — appreciates opinionated, well-designed tools

### Secondary ICP: The Network-Dependent Solo Professional
- Freelancers, investors, consultants, salespeople
- Revenue directly tied to relationships
- Needs to track warm introductions, follow-ups, and key contact milestones
- Has tried Salesforce / HubSpot — too corporate, wrong context

### Tertiary ICP: The Community Organizer
- Runs meetups, Discord servers, coworking spaces
- Tracks dozens of relationships across WhatsApp, spreadsheets, memory
- Needs multi-user vaults and easy contact sharing
- Values self-hosted privacy

## 3. Three Key Differentiators

### Differentiator 1: Agent-First Architecture
Monica and Twenty are **tools** — you remember to open them. SOCOS is an **agent** — it remembers *for* you.

- **Reminder Agent:** Checks your contacts every morning, flags who needs attention, fires birthday/anniversary reminders proactively
- **Enrichment Agent:** Auto-fills contact info from the web when you add someone
- **Summary Agent:** Before you meet someone, generates a recap of your last conversation and key facts
- **Suggestion Agent:** "You haven't messaged Alex in 47 days. Want to schedule coffee?"

**Messaging:** *"Stop maintaining a contact list. Let your AI maintain your relationships."*

---

### Differentiator 2: Gamification Engine
Most people *want* to stay in touch but don't because it feels like admin work with no reward.

- **XP per interaction:** +10 for a logged call, +20 for completing a reminder, +50 for a new contact
- **Levels 1–100:** Social Novice → Network Builder → Relationship Master → Connection Virtuoso
- **Streaks:** 30-day messaging streak = 🔥
- **Achievements:** First Contact, Birthday Pro, Streak Master, Quality Time

**Messaging:** *"Relationship skills are real skills. Level up."*

---

### Differentiator 3: Privacy-First, Open Source
Your relationship data is the most personal data you have. It shouldn't be in a corporate CRM.

- Self-hosted by default (Docker, one-command deploy)
- Open source, MIT license
- No ads, no data selling, no vendor lock-in
- API-first design — AI agents can interact with it autonomously

**Messaging:** *"Your data belongs to you. Your relationships belong to you."*

## 4. Social CRM / Personal CRM Positioning

SOCOS sits at the intersection of two markets:

- **Personal CRM** (Monica, Apple Contacts, Notion templates) — stores contact data
- **Social CRM** (Twitter/LinkedIn tracking, community tools) — tracks social interactions

**SOCOS's angle:** Neither market has solved the *maintenance* problem. Contacts go stale. Reminders are manual. SOCOS closes the loop with AI agents + gamification to make maintenance rewarding, not dutiful.

**Target keyword territory:** personal CRM, relationship tracker, friendship manager, AI personal assistant, social skills tracker, contact health score

## 6. Brand Voice Guidelines

**Tone:** Warmly encouraging, smartly playful. Think: your most organized friend who also sends you memes.

**Do:**
- ✅ Celebrate small wins (logging a check-in = a win!)
- ✅ Acknowledge that building relationships is hard
- ✅ Use conversational language — contractions, first person plural
- ✅ Make gamification feel rewarding, not childish

**Don't:**
- ❌ Corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, ecosystem)
- ❌ Preachy relationship messaging
- ❌ Excessive exclamation marks
- ❌ Stiff or formal tone
