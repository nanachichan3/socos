## Positioning Statement (One-Liner)

> **SOCOS is the AI-powered personal CRM that turns relationship-building into a rewarding game — for people who want to grow their circle, not their business.**

---

## Target Persona

### Primary Persona: The Relationship Intentionalist

**Who they are:**
- **Age:** 25–45
- **Life stage:** Early-to-mid career, recently moved to a new city, or entering a phase where networking matters (job switch, freelance, entrepreneurship)
- **Psychographic:** They value deep friendships and meaningful connections but feel they're losing touch with people. They use a notes app or memory to track contacts because no tool feels right for *personal* relationship management.
- **Tech-savvy:** Comfortable with productivity tools, self-hosts some things, appreciates open-source

**Pain points:**
- Forgets to follow up with people they care about
- Can't remember how they know someone or what they last talked about
- Feels guilty about letting friendships fade
- Has tried spreadsheet hacks or generic contact managers but nothing stuck
- Wants a tool that actually *reminds* and *helps* them — not just stores data

**Current solutions:**
- Apple Contacts / Google Contacts (dumps contacts, no reminders, no context)
- Notion templates (too manual, no automation)
- Monica CRM (too business-focused, no gamification, feels like a sales tool)
- Sticky notes and memory (inconsistent, not searchable)

**Trigger moment:** They realize they've lost touch with someone important, or they've just moved somewhere new and want to be intentional about building their social circle.

---

### Secondary Persona: The Growth-Minded Networker

**Who they are:**
- Active on LinkedIn/Twitter, attends events, conferences
- Tracks business contacts but doesn't want a *sales* tool
- Interested in self-improvement and gamification

**Pain points:**
- Has contacts scattered across LinkedIn, business cards, phone
- Wants to follow up but doesn't have a system
- Finds existing CRMs too corporate and cold

**Current solutions:** Spreadsheets, LinkedIn contacts, mental tracking

**1. You shouldn't have to remember alone.**
> SOCOS's AI agents remember every conversation, track every relationship, and nudge you when it's time to reach out — so you never lose touch with people who matter.

**2. Building relationships is a skill — level it up.**
> Like a fitness tracker for your social life, SOCOS gamifies every interaction. Earn XP, unlock achievements, build streaks. Growth feels good.

> Self-hosted, open-source, privacy-first. SOCOS runs on your server, your rules. No ads, no data selling, no corporate surveillance.

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone: Warmly Encouraging, Smartly Playful

SOCOS sounds like a supportive friend who's also a bit of a nerd. Not corporate. Not clinical. Not hype-y.

**Do:**
- ✅ Speak like a helpful guide, not a sales rep
- ✅ Celebrate small wins (logging a check-in = a win!)
- ✅ Acknowledge that building relationships is hard
- ✅ Use conversational language — contractions, first person
- ✅ Make gamification feel rewarding, not childish

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use corporate buzzwords like synergy, leverage, ecosystem
- ❌ Be preachy about relationship importance
- ❌ Talk down to users
- ❌ Use exclamation marks excessively
- ❌ Be stiff or formal

### Voice Examples

**Good:**
> *Your contact Sarah hasn't heard from you in 42 days. Want to send a quick hello? It counts toward your streak.*

**Bad:**
> *RECONNECT WITH DORMANT CONTACTS NOW! Don't let relationships die! Activate your outreach strategy today!*

**Good:**
> *You leveled up! Level 12: Network Builder. You're building something good here.*

**Bad:**
> *Achievement Unlocked! Congratulations on your continued engagement with SOCOS CRM™.*

### Writing Principles

1. **Human-first:** Write like a person, not a marketing department
2. **Specific > Vague:** Use real numbers, real contexts, real examples
3. **Encourage action:** Don't command — invite
4. **Keep it light:** Humor is welcome, especially around gamification
5. **Stay honest:** Don't oversell. SOCOS helps, it doesn't do the work for you
