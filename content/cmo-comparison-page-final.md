## The Problem with Other CRMs

Most CRMs are built for one of two things: **sales pipelines** or **contact databases**. They store information. They don't help you maintain relationships.

- Monica is a solid database — but you do all the work
- Twenty is built for B2B sales — not personal connection
- Neither has an intelligence layer. You're the agent.

SOCOS is different. It's the first personal CRM with **AI agents that actively manage your relationships** — reminding you, enriching contacts, and keeping streaks alive. Combined with gamification that makes consistency feel rewarding.

Monica stores. Twenty sells. **SOCOS maintains.**

## The Agent-First Difference

### Monica — You do the work
Monica is a solid relationship database. You add contacts, log interactions, set reminders. But it's entirely manual. There's no intelligence layer. No automatic enrichment. No proactive nudges.

If you stop using it, it stops working. Your relationships don't get maintained — they just get stored.

### Twenty — Built for deals, not people
Twenty has solid infrastructure and an API-first approach. But it's built for B2B sales teams tracking deals. The "automations" are workflow triggers, not proactive AI agents. It's powerful for a sales team. Less useful for maintaining your college roommate's contact info.

### SOCOS — Agents that work for you
SOCOS has a full agent stack built on LangChain:

- **Relationship Agent**: Monitors contact health, scores relationship vitality, surfaces dormant contacts
- **Reminder Agent**: Auto-creates birthday and check-in reminders based on interaction history
- **Enrichment Agent**: Pulls public profile data to auto-complete contact records
- **Summary Agent**: Generates a recap before you reconnect
- **Suggestion Agent**: Recommends people you should add based on shared interests or mutual connections

**The key difference**: SOCOS agents work *for* you between sessions. You don't have to open the app to benefit from it.

## Three Use Cases

### The Networker

**Alex**, indie hacker, 200+ contacts across events, Twitter, and real life. Knows they should follow up more but keeps forgetting.

With SOCOS:
- Relationship Agent flags that Alex hasn't contacted Jordan in 47 days
- Suggests a check-in. Alex responds: "Hey, it's been a while — want to catch up?"
- Alex logs the interaction: +10 XP, streak continues
- 3 days later, Jordan's birthday auto-populates in reminders
- Alex feels like a better friend without adding mental overhead

**Result**: Alex maintains relationships they would have otherwise let go dormant. The app does the tracking; Alex does the connecting.

---

### The Relationship Manager

**Priya**, small business owner, 200+ client contacts. Needs to stay top-of-mind without letting anyone fall through the cracks.

With SOCOS:
- Every client contact has a relationship health score
- Reminder Agent auto-generates check-in reminders based on last interaction + client importance
- Enrichment Agent pulls LinkedIn context so Priya remembers how she knows someone
- XP gamifies the follow-up process — she actually enjoys logging check-ins
- Self-hosted: her client data never touches a corporate server

**Result**: Priya manages 200+ client relationships without a single reminder she didn't set herself. The agents handle the tracking; she handles the relationship.

---

### The Solo Founder

**Jordan**, solo founder, building in public. Needs to nurture warm leads, press contacts, and early adopters — without a sales team.

With SOCOS:
- Warm leads get their own label and relationship tracking
- Suggestion Agent flags people Jordan should reconnect with based on interaction history
- Summary Agent generates a recap before every reconnection: "Last time you spoke, they asked about your launch timeline"
- Streak system keeps Jordan accountable for daily relationship maintenance — even when building takes over
- Reminder Agent surfaces dormant leads before they go cold

**Result**: Jordan never lets a warm lead go cold again. The agents handle the reminders; Jordan handles the conversations.
